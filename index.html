OUR COLLECTION



Gyaku Choco


Gyaku in Japanese means "reversal". Thus, not only women give chocolate to men as in the Japanese Valentine's tradition, men also have the opportunity to express their affection by giving female friends Gyaku Choco.

Honmei Choco


Honmei Choco is a fragrant milk chocolate with a distinctive sweet taste, often shaped into a heart. Honmei means true love, if you receive this type of chocolate, person giving you is intending to express more than friendship.

Jiko Choco


Jiko Choco is the most special chocolate on Valentine's Day in Japan. Normal chocolates that Chefjob just shared are used to express love for men and women, Jiko Choco is specifically for those who are single.

Tomo Choco


Tomo has the meaning of friend, similar to Cho-giri Choco but means more respect and love. This can also be seen as a message: "I only consider you a friend." Girls often give this chocolate with a card that says "For my friend".
